# Fivefive — Brand brief

> One-page reference for how Fivefive looks, sounds, and behaves.
> Use this brief as input when generating wordmark + logo mark variations.

---

## 1 · What is Fivefive

A multiplayer Sequence-style board game for phones and laptops. Play with friends or against bots, anywhere a browser opens. Fast to start, smart to play.

**Domain**: play.fivefive.app
**Game ™ note**: Sequence™ is a registered trademark of Jax Ltd. Fivefive is an unofficial online platform for the game.

The name **fivefive** comes from:
- Five in a row = the win condition
- Five-five = the rhythm — two halves of a game, a high-five after locking a sequence, the chip-line clapping into place

---

## 2 · Voice & tone

**Confident, playful, considered.** Like a friend who plays well but doesn't gloat.

| | Voice | Anti-voice |
|---|-------|------------|
| Tone | Direct, warm, smart | Aggressive · childish · pretentious · corporate |
| Style | Short sentences. Real verbs. | Filler words. Marketing speak. |
| Mood | Welcoming with edge | Patronizing or sterile |

**Voice samples** (use these as reference):

- "Your move." — never "Hey there, friend! It's your turn now!"
- "Sequence locked." — never "Way to go! You scored! Whoo!"
- "Tough day at the table. Faizi has notes." — never "Don't give up! Try again!"
- "Wolfpak wins. MVP: Ayesha — 4 sequence-closing moves."
- "Tap a card to see where it can play. Tap a glowing square to drop a chip."
- "Faizi here. I watched every move. Want my read?"

---

## 3 · Brand pillars

The three filters every design + copy decision passes through.

1. **Fast** — From open to first move in under 10 seconds. No friction nobody asked for.
2. **Smart** — Real chess-like depth. Faizi exists. Bots punish hesitation.
3. **Social** — Designed for a table. Quick chat, stickers, replays, roasts. Solo play is the side door.

---

## 4 · What Fivefive is NOT

To stay honest:

- ❌ **Not a kids' app.** Friendly, not childish. We use real words.
- ❌ **Not a casino app.** No paid chips, no slot-machine vibes, no real money.
- ❌ **Not a generic gamer aesthetic.** No gradients-everywhere RGB, no cyberpunk neons, no Fortnite jargon.
- ❌ **Not slow.** Long loading states, walls of text, and tutorial pop-ups are the enemy.
- ❌ **Not corporate.** No SaaS-grid landing pages with feature checkmarks.

## 5 · Visual identity

**Personality direction: Royal navy + cartoon.**

A grown-up navy poker-room baseline (deep blue + champagne gold + cream) is the floor. On top of that we apply a sticker-style cartoon language — thick dark outlines, hard offset drop shadows, generous rounding, gentle rotations. The base says "this is a real game played by real players." The cartoon says "we're not taking ourselves too seriously."

### Color palette

| Role | Token | Hex | Use |
|------|-------|-----|-----|
| Surface · base | `navy` | `#0a1840` | Page background |
| Surface · raised | `navy-soft` | `#122560` | Card backgrounds |
| Surface · card | `navy-card` | `#1a2f78` | Hero, win overlay |
| Outline | `navy-line` | `#060d2a` | All outlines + drop shadows |
| Brand primary | `gold` | `#e4c373` | Primary accent · brand · MVP · highlights |
| Brand primary deep | `gold-deep` | `#c9a35b` | Gold gradient bottom, hover states |
| Body text | `cream` | `#fef3c7` | All text on dark surfaces |
| Body text soft | `cream-soft` | `#fde68a` | Secondary text |
| Accent · warm | `coral` | `#fb7185` | Red team · alerts · "Stop game" · 2nd-CTA |
| Accent · success | `mint` | `#86efac` | Confirmations · "earned" checkmarks |
| Accent · cool | `sky` | `#38bdf8` | Blue team · informational |
| Accent · danger | `coral-deep` | `#e11d48` | Destructive actions |

Background uses radial-gradient glows in gold + coral + sky at very low opacity (5-8%) — feels lit from within.

### Typography

| Use | Family | Weight | Tracking |
|-----|--------|--------|----------|
| Display (logo, "SEQUENCE!", win team) | **Bagel Fat One** | 400 (only weight) | -0.02em |
| Titles, headings, button labels | **Fredoka** | 500-700 | normal |
| Body, UI copy | **Inter** | 400-700 | normal |
| Numbers, handles, code | **Geist Mono** | 400 | normal |

All free Google Fonts. Variable where possible.

### Visual language — "elevated cartoon"

Six rules that make Fivefive feel like Fivefive:

1. **3px dark navy outlines** (`#060d2a`) on every interactive surface (cards, buttons, badges, chips). Outlines stay constant — they don't get thinner at small sizes.
2. **Hard offset drop shadows** — `4px 4px 0 var(--navy-line)` with **zero blur**. Bigger on hover (translates -2px), snaps inward on click (translates +1px).
3. **Generous rounding** — 20px on cards, 14px on small cells, 999px (full pill) on buttons and badges.
4. **Subtle hand-placed rotations** — gold accents tilt -3°, chat bubbles alternate ±2°, Faizi's avatar tilts -4°. Nothing sits perfectly square.
5. **Small confetti motifs** — outlined circles in gold / coral / sky / mint scattered as background decoration on hero and win surfaces.
6. **Gentle ambient motion** — current player bounces 3px every 1.2s, trophy wobbles ±6° every 2s, chat bubbles float ±4px. Easing: standard ease-in-out. Nothing loops faster than 1.2s.

### Game-piece exception

**The chips in the game stay as currently designed.** Flat solid fill, 2.5px outline, 1px 1.5px hard drop shadow, 56% cell width. The cartoon language applies to everything AROUND the chips — the cards, the cells, the board frame, the score panels — not the chips themselves.

## 6 · Wordmark brief

The wordmark is the typeset rendering of "fivefive" — what shows in the hero, the navigation, social profiles, t-shirts.

### Requirements

- **The word**: `fivefive` (one word, all lowercase)
- **Aspect ratio**: roughly 4:1 (wide and short)
- **Readable at**: 16px tall minimum (favicon-adjacent)
- **Works on**: dark navy AND on light cream backgrounds (provide both versions)
- **Color**: gold primary; alternate in cream for use on gold surfaces
- **Letter spacing**: tight (-0.02em to -0.04em) — letters should hug
- **Feel**: chunky, friendly, confident — NOT thin, NOT calligraphic, NOT all-caps

### Variations to generate (5–8 candidates)

Try a spread across these axes. Pick a base direction per variation, not all at once.

| # | Direction | Specifics |
|---|-----------|-----------|
| A | **Bagel Fat One (default)** | Use it as-is. Letterforms are already chunky + rounded. |
| B | **Hand-drawn marker** | Looks like the wordmark was painted with a thick marker on felt. Slightly imperfect edges. |
| C | **Custom geometric** | Built from circles and rounded rectangles. Each letter ~equal width. |
| D | **Ligature emphasis** | The two "f"s connect via a top crossbar. Or the two "v"s share a vertex. |
| E | **Number swap** | Replace the two "fives" with "5"s where they appear: `5ve5ve` (or `5★ve5★ve`). Reads as a pictogram. |
| F | **Italic / leaning** | Wordmark leans -8° to -12° right, like it's mid-action. |
| G | **Outlined-only** | Letters are just thick outlines, fillable with any brand color depending on surface. |
| H | **Stacked half** | `five` stacks on top of `five`, creating a square logo block — for square avatars / favicons. |

### What to AVOID

- Serif faces (Playfair, Times) — too formal for the cartoon layer
- Mono faces — feels too code-y
- All-caps treatments — feels shouty, not playful
- Drop shadows on the wordmark itself (the navy text-shadow we use in the hero is a contextual effect, not part of the wordmark file)
- Gradients across letters in the locked wordmark (gradient backgrounds are fine; the wordmark stays solid)

### Deliverable format

For each variation:
- SVG (vector, with letters as paths, not live text)
- 1× PNG at 512px wide on transparent background
- 1× PNG at 512px wide on `#0a1840` navy
- Single-color lockup: gold (`#e4c373`) version + cream (`#fef3c7`) version

## 7 · Logo mark brief

The mark is the standalone icon — used as favicon, PWA icon, social profile photo, in-app chip-sized brand bug. Must work without the wordmark.

### Requirements

- **Shape constraints**: fits a rounded square (24px radius), 1:1 aspect ratio
- **Maskable safe area**: keep content within central 80% (Android masks corners to circles/squircles)
- **Recognizable at**: 32×32 px (browser tab favicon)
- **Color**: full color version (gold + navy + accent) AND single-color version (gold only, or navy only)
- **Feel**: confident pictogram. Reads at a glance.

### Concept directions (pick 1–3 to explore)

| # | Concept | Sketch description |
|---|---------|--------------------|
| 1 | **Two 5s mirrored** | Two "5" digits, one normal + one mirrored, forming a symmetric stamp. Numbers face each other like a mirror. Reads as "55" both ways. |
| 2 | **5-dot quincunx** | Five filled circles in the standard die-face-five pattern (the pattern on a 5-domino). Two of those squares tiled together = "fivefive". |
| 3 | **Five chips in a row** | A horizontal row of 5 filled chips, the middle one slightly larger (the "winning" chip). Says "sequence" pictographically. |
| 4 | **"55" lockup** | The numbers "55" in Bagel Fat One, set inside the rounded gold square, with a small coral chip-dot accent at the corner. (This is the version in the current sample.) |
| 5 | **Chip-with-arrows** | A single chip in gold, with four small arrows pointing outward in the four diagonal directions (the four winning lines of Sequence). |
| 6 | **Pentagram / 5-point** | The number 5 as a star inscribed in a circle, with chip-like outlines. Subtle nod to "five." |

### Color treatment

For each shortlisted concept, provide:
- **Full color**: gold mark on navy square, with at least one accent (coral dot, mint check, etc.)
- **Mono gold**: mark in gold only, navy or transparent background
- **Mono navy**: mark in navy only, gold or transparent background
- **White/cream**: for use on dark photo backgrounds

### Construction rules

- All strokes: 4–6% of icon width (consistent visual weight)
- All corners: rounded, never sharp (matches cartoon language)
- Optical adjustments: corners may be slightly thinner than mid-stroke (compensate for visual heavy spots)
- 8px grid — keep all anchor points on round numbers when possible

### What to AVOID

- Realistic dice / poker chips with shading and gradients (too literal)
- Card-game suit symbols (♠♥♦♣) — those belong to the cards, not the brand
- Anything that requires text to "read" (we use the wordmark for that)
- Tiny details that disappear under 32px

## 9 · Tone of "no" — when to push back on design suggestions

If a design choice violates these, push back:

- Adds a stroke thinner than 2px (we go bold or we don't go)
- Adds a gradient with more than 3 stops (gradient-soup territory)
- Adds a font that's not in our 4-family stack without a clear reason
- Adds drop shadows with non-zero blur (we use hard offset shadows only)
- Sets text in all-caps for body or UI (uppercase is for tiny meta-labels only: section headers, badge labels)
- Animates anything looping faster than 800ms (chaos)
- Uses real-money / gambling iconography (we are not a casino app)
